Hollywood on Fire

Disaster struck California right at the beginning of 2025. Wild fires erupted on January 7th, 2025 starting as a brush fire in the Palisades.

The flames were fed by strong winds and dry weather conditions and spread to over 23,448 acres of land in the Palisades.

The fires started in the Palisades of California on the morning of January 7th and then spread the storm through to the Eaton area just hours after the initial flames.

Evacuation orders were given up to 200,000 people in the areas effected.





Damages and Destruction Aftermath

Confirmed by medical examiner's office as well as the California Department of Forestry and Fire Protection, the death toll has risen to 29 souls.

Approximately, according to NBC News and Cal Fire, over 37,000 acres of land burned.



Monday after the fires broke out, some residents were allowed to return to their homes and neighborhoods. Some victimes, unfortunately, ended up homeless due to the wreckage of the disaster.

NBC News reports that JPMorgan has estimated anwhere from $20 billion to $50 billion in losses or claims to losses from the fires.

The cause of the fires are still under investigation. Controversy strikes political spaces as Mayor Karen Bass fires the L.A. Fire Chief Kristin Crowley after the fires took place.





Timeline of the Fires

January 7th - 10:30 a.m.

Fire begins to burn in the Southeast of the Palisades starting at 10 acres

January 7th - 12:30 p.m.

Palisades fire quickly grew to 200 acres, evacuation orders given for those in the fires path to Pacific Coast Highway

January 7th - 6:30 p.m.

Fire starts in Altadena where the Eaton Fires begin, 80 mph winds rising, two fires are now being fought by firefighters

January 7th - 10:30 p.m.

Third fire breaks out in Sylmar as the Hurst Fire, 800 acres begins to burn

January 8th - 9:00 a.m.

Both Palisades and Eaton fires double in size, firefighters continue their efforts

January 24th

More than two weeks later the fires are noted to be 100% contained. Over 37,000 acres of burned land and homes.
